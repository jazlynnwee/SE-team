# Operations Discovery Reference — For SE Awareness

This is a reference of the kinds of operational details the implementation team finds valuable when starting a new project. It's not a checklist for the SE to complete during sales — it's context for what kinds of information are useful to pick up naturally through discovery calls, on-site visits, and stakeholder conversations.

The SE's primary job is to sell well and build trust. But since SEs are already having deep operational conversations, they're in a great position to pick up details that make the post-sale handoff smoother. The more the SE absorbs organically, the better — but no one expects every box to be covered.

---

## When These Topics Come Up Naturally

**Early conversations and first on-site visits** tend to surface: project goals, KPIs, service area, and the big-picture service parameters. These also happen to be the things that make your demos sharper and your business case stronger.

**Mid-cycle conversations with dispatchers, supervisors, and operators** tend to surface: fleet configuration, scheduling, fares, and notification setup. This is the stuff you pick up when you shadow operations.

**Later-stage and pre-close conversations** sometimes surface: data export readiness, reporting gaps, and historical metrics. These are more tactical and signal to the prospect that you're already thinking about what comes next.

## Category Reference

### 1. Project Goals & KPIs
- What problems is the agency/operator trying to solve?
- What does success look like 6-12 months post-launch?
- Biggest challenges with current service or software
- Where staff or riders experience the most friction
- KPIs tracked today (e.g., OTP, PPVH, PPRH, TPRH) with current values and targets

### 2. Service Area & Travel Rules
- Description of service area(s) and whether shapefiles exist
- Travel rules: city/county boundaries, cross-jurisdiction, rider-type-specific rules
- Restricted areas, OSA (outside service area) grandfathered riders

### 3. Service Parameters
- Booking rules: advance window, cutoff time, pickup window
- Boarding/alighting time standards (including by mobility aid type)
- Subscription/recurring trip rules and percentage of total
- Service access: open to public or approval/eligibility-based

### 4. Time Rules & Holidays
- Days and hours of operation
- Whether rules vary by area or service type
- Holiday operations

### 5. Fares & Payments
- Fare model: per passenger, per ride, or variable
- Fare variations by rider type, discounts, eligibility
- Payment methods: cash, credit/debit, third-party, vouchers, on-board vs. pre-pay

### 6. Vehicles & Fleet Configuration
- Vehicle types, quantities, and configurations
- Mobility aid capacity and how it varies by vehicle
- How vehicles are assigned: pooled vs. zone-specific
- Any vehicle-type-specific travel rules
- CNG, electric, or specialty vehicles

### 7. Rider Notifications
- Current channels: SMS, phone, email, IVR
- Notification types: booking confirmation, reminders, vehicle arriving, cancellations
- What they wish they could send but can't today

### 8. Scheduling & Driver Duties
- Run schedule structure and frequency of run cuts
- Driver assignment cadence: weekly, daily
- Vehicle-to-duty assignment timing
- Break rules and enforcement

### 9. Reporting & Compliance
- Reports used regularly and reporting gaps
- FTA/NTD reporting requirements
- Client (agency) reporting requirements for the operator

### 10. Data & Migration
- Rider, driver, vehicle, trip, and schedule data exportability
- 12 months of historical trip data availability
- Current system's API or export capabilities

### 11. Subcontractor & TNC Integration
- Which subcontractors/TNCs are used (taxis, Uber/Lyft, UZURV, WAV providers)
- How trips are sent to subs (API, export, manual)
- How sub trip status flows back
- Billing and payment structure with subs

### 12. Operational Metrics (Last 12 Months)
- Ridership, OTP, vehicle/revenue hours and distance
- Productivity: PPVH, PPRH, TPVH, TPRH
- Cost per hour/trip, no-shows, missed trips, denials
- Call center: volume, duration, ASA, AHT, abandoned %, CSAT
